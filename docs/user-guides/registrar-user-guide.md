# Registrar User Guide

## Overview
This guide covers admissions, enrollment, sectioning/scheduling, records maintenance, transcripts, transfers/withdrawals, and compliance reporting.

## Audience & Permissions
- Audience: Registrars and admissions staff
- Access: Registrar module with student records and scheduling controls

## Prerequisites
- Active registrar account
- Academic term configured

## Getting Started
1. Sign in to `Registrar Portal`.
2. Verify admissions queue and enrollment pipeline.

![Registrar Dashboard](images/registrar/dashboard.png)

## Core Tasks

### Admissions & Enrollment
1. Navigate to `Admissions`.
2. Review applications and required documents.
3. Change status: Applied → Admitted → Enrolled.
4. Trigger welcome notifications.

![Admissions](images/registrar/admissions.png)

### Records Maintenance
1. Open `Student Records`.
2. Update personal info, guardians, and program details.
3. Maintain audit trail for changes.

### Sectioning & Scheduling
1. Navigate to `Scheduling`.
2. Assign students to sections; resolve conflicts.
3. Publish schedules; notify students and teachers.

![Scheduling](images/registrar/scheduling.png)

### Document Issuance
1. Issue IDs, enrollment certificates, and clearance forms.
2. Generate PDFs and record issuance.

### Transcripts
1. Open `Reports > Transcripts`.
2. Validate grades and compute GPA.
3. Generate official transcript with signatures.

### Transfers & Withdrawals
1. Process transfer-in/out; collect required documents.
2. Handle withdrawals with approval and fee adjustments.

### Compliance Reporting
1. Generate required government or accreditation reports.
2. Export and archive submissions.

## Troubleshooting
- Missing document: request upload via admissions portal.
- Schedule conflict: use conflict solver and alternative sections.

## FAQs
- How to reissue transcript? Use `Reports > Transcripts > Reprint`.
- Can I bulk-assign sections? Use `Bulk Sectioning` tools.

## Security Notes
- Maintain data accuracy and protect PII; use audit logs for changes.
