# Welcome, Librarian! 📚

As a Librarian in the Coldea Management Information System, you are responsible for managing the library's book collection, tracking borrowing activities, and maintaining the library's inventory. This guide will help you efficiently manage library operations and provide excellent service to students and faculty.

## 🎯 Your Role Overview

**Access Level**: Librarian Access  
**Key Responsibilities**: Manage book inventory, track borrowing records, maintain library resources, and assist library users

### What You Can Access:
- ✅ **Dashboard**: Library operations overview
- ✅ **Books**: Complete book inventory management
- ✅ **Book Management**: Add, edit, and remove books
- ✅ **Borrowing Records**: Track all borrowing activities
- ✅ **Library Reports**: Generate library statistics and reports
- ✅ **User Profiles**: View student and faculty library information
- ✅ **Inventory Management**: Track book availability and condition

### What You Cannot Access:
- ❌ Student academic records (grades, enrollments)
- ❌ Financial information (payments, fees)
- ❌ Administrative functions (user management)
- ❌ Course and schedule management
- ❌ System settings and configuration

## 🚀 Getting Started

### Step 1: Complete Your Librarian Profile
1. Click on **"My Profile"** in the sidebar
2. Update your professional information:
   - Contact details for library inquiries
   - Professional qualifications
   - Library specializations
   - Working hours and availability
3. Set up your librarian preferences

### Step 2: Explore Your Dashboard
Your librarian dashboard provides:
- **Library Overview**: Total books, available books, borrowed books
- **Recent Activities**: Latest borrowing and return activities
- **Overdue Items**: Books that need attention
- **Popular Books**: Most frequently borrowed items
- **Quick Actions**: Common library management tasks

### Step 3: Familiarize Yourself with the Book Collection
1. Navigate to **Books** to see the complete inventory
2. Review book categories and classifications
3. Check book availability status
4. Understand the cataloging system

## 💡 Pro Tips

1. **Daily Inventory**: Start each day by checking overdue items and returns
2. **Book Condition**: Regularly inspect books for damage during returns
3. **Popular Tracking**: Monitor popular books to anticipate demand
4. **Student Assistance**: Be proactive in helping students find resources
